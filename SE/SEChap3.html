SOFTWARE ENGINEERING ACTIVITIES

Software engineering activities involve the coordination of people, product development, project management, and process implementation to create successful software products.

Software engineering activities, especially for large systems, require the coordination of many people (stakeholders) who must be arranged into teams. Their primary objective is to build a product that meets defined requirements. The entire effort must be organized into a cohesive project with a solid plan for success. Finally, to successfully develop the product, the activities of the people must be organized through use of an orderly and well-defined process.

People in Software Engineering

The most important resource on a software project and the lifeblood of any successful project. Several groups of people (stakeholders) have a stake in a project's outcome, including business management, project management, the development team, customers, and end users.

Product in Software Engineering

The product consists of much more than just source and object code. It also includes documentation, test plans and results, customer documentation or manuals, and productivity measurements. These products are called artifacts.

Project in Software Engineering

A software project defines the activities and associated results needed to produce a software product. Every project involves a similar set of activities: planning, determining requirements, determining how the software should be built, implementing the software, testing, and maintaining it.

Process in Software Engineering

A software process is a framework for carrying out the activities of a project in an organized and disciplined manner. It imposes structure and helps guide the many people and activities in a coherent manner. Specific software process implementations are called software process models.

PEOPLE

People are the most important resource on a software project and are the lifeblood of any successful project.

Several groups of people are involved with and have a stake in a project's outcome. These are called stakeholders. Stakeholders include business management, project management, the development team, customers, and end users.

Business Management

  • People responsible for the business side of the company developing the software.
  • Primary focus is on business issues including profit, cost effectiveness, market competitiveness, and customer satisfaction.
  • Are typically not particularly knowledgeable about or involved in the technical aspects of the project.
  • Includes senior management, marketing, and development management.

Project Management

  • Are responsible for planning and tracking a project.
  • Are involved throughout managing the people, process, and activities.
  • Continuously monitor progress and proactively implement necessary changes and improvements to keep the project on schedule and within budget.

Development Team

  • Software engineers are responsible for developing and maintaining the software.
  • Handles requirements gathering, software architecture and design, implementation, testing, configuration management, and documentation.

Customers

  • Responsible for purchasing the software.
  • They may or may not actually use the software. They may be purchasing it for use by others in their organization.
  • Primarily interested in software that is cost-effective, meets specific business needs, and is of high quality.
  • Involved in some aspects of specifying requirements, and since they are paying for the project, they have the ultimate say in defining the requirements.

End-Users

  • End users are people who interact with and use software after it is finished being developed.
  • Motivated by software that is easy to use and helps them perform their jobs as efficiently as possible.

Key Takeaway

People are the most critical element in software engineering projects. Understanding the roles, responsibilities, and motivations of different stakeholders is essential for successful project execution.

PRODUCT

The product of a software development effort consists of much more than the source and object code.

Software products also include documentation, test plans and results, customer documentation or manuals, and productivity measurements. These products are called artifacts.

Main Product Artifacts of a Software Project

  • Project documentation: Documents produced during software definition and development.
  • Code: Source and object. (Source code = Human readable, Object code = Machine readable)
  • Test documents: Plans, cases, and results.
  • Customer documents: Documents explaining how to use and operate the product.
  • Productivity measurements: Analyze project productivity.

Key Takeaway

The complete software product includes not just code, but also various types of documentation and measurements that contribute to the overall quality and usefulness of the software.

PROJECT

A software project defines the activities and associated results needed to produce a software product.

Every project involves a similar set of activities: planning, determining what's required, determining how the software should be built to meet the requirements, implementing the software, testing the software, and maintaining it once delivered to customers.

Major Activities of a Software Project

  • Planning: Plan, monitor, and control the software project.
  • Requirements analysis: Define what to build.
  • Design: Describe how to build the software.
  • Implementation: Program the software.
  • Testing: Validate that software meets the requirements.
  • Deployment: Install the software for client use.
  • Maintenance: Resolve problems, adapt software to meet new requirements.

Key Takeaway

A software project is a structured approach to developing software, involving a series of well-defined activities from planning to maintenance that must be carefully managed to ensure success.

PROCESS

A software process is a framework for carrying out the activities of a project in an organized and disciplined manner.

It imposes structure and helps guide the many people and activities in a coherent manner. A software project progresses through different phases, each interrelated and bounded by time and a software process expresses the interrelationships among the phases by defining their order and frequency, as well as defining the deliverables of the project. Specific software process implementations are called software process models.

Best Practice

Choosing the right software process model for a project is crucial for its success. The process should be tailored to the specific needs, constraints, and goals of the project.

Key Takeaway

A well-defined software process provides structure and guidance to the project, ensuring that activities are carried out in a coherent and disciplined manner.
